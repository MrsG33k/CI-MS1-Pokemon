What is a Pokédex?

The Pokédex is a high-tech device that records information about Pokémon you've encountered and caught. Each Pokémon has a unique entry with its number, type, and description. Trainers use the Pokédex to track their journey and learn more about the Pokémon world!

Let's see which Pokémon make up the Kanto Generation!
Bulbasaur
  • #001
  • A Seed Pokémon that bears a plant bulb on its back, which grows into a large plant as it evolves.
Ivysaur
  • #002
  • The evolved form of Bulbasaur; its plant bulb blooms into a flower bud as it prepares for further evolution.
Venusaur
  • #003
  • A fully evolved form of Bulbasaur with a large flower on its back that absorbs sunlight to convert into energy.
Charmander
  • #004
  • A Lizard Pokémon known for the flame on its tail, which indicates its life force.
Charmeleon
  • #005
  • An aggressive Pokémon that lashes out with its fiery tail when angered.
Charizard
  • #006
  • Spits fire hot enough to melt boulders and is known to unintentionally cause forest fires.
Squirtle
  • #007
  • A Tiny Turtle Pokémon that shelters itself in its shell and sprays water at opponents.
Wartortle
  • #008
  • Recognized by its fluffy tail, it is a skilled swimmer that uses its tail to maintain balance.
Blastoise
  • #009
  • Equipped with water cannons on its shell, it can deliver high-pressure blasts to its targets.
Caterpie
  • #010
  • A Worm Pokémon that releases a foul odor from its antenna to repel predators.
Metapod
  • #011
  • Encased within a hard shell, it prepares for evolution by remaining motionless.
Butterfree
  • #012
  • Flutters in search of honey, spreading pollen that can cause allergic reactions.
Weedle
  • #013
  • A Hairy Bug Pokémon with a venomous stinger on its head to fend off attackers.
Kakuna
  • #014
  • While immobile, it is in a transitional stage, preparing to evolve into Beedrill.
Beedrill
  • #015
  • A Poison Bee Pokémon that flies at high speed and attacks with its sharp stingers.
Pidgey
  • #016
  • A docile bird Pokémon that is commonly found in forests and grasslands.
Pidgeotto
  • #017
  • It claims a large area as its territory and will fiercely defend it from intruders.
Pidgeot
  • #018
  • Known for its speed, it can fly at Mach 2 and creates shockwaves with its wings.
Rattata
  • #019
  • A Mouse Pokémon that gnaws on anything with its ever-growing incisors.
Raticate
  • #020
  • Its powerful hind legs enable it to jump great distances to catch prey.
Spearow
  • #021
  • A Tiny Bird Pokémon with a loud cry that can be heard over half a mile away.
Fearow
  • #022
  • With its long neck and beak, it plucks prey from both land and water.
Ekans
  • #023
  • A Snake Pokémon that quietly slithers through grass, hunting its prey.
Arbok
  • #024
  • The frightening patterns on its belly have been known to cause paralysis.
Pikachu
  • #025
  • A Mouse Pokémon that stores electricity in its cheeks, releasing it in lightning-based attacks.
Raichu
  • #026
  • When electricity builds up inside its body, it becomes feisty. It also glows in the dark.
Sandshrew
  • #027
  • Burrows deep underground to stay cool and dry. Its body is dry when it emerges.
Sandslash
  • #028
  • Curls up into a spiny ball when threatened. Its claws are sharp and can slice through thick materials.
Nidoran♀
  • #029
  • A mild-mannered Pokémon that does not like to fight. Small, but packs a powerful poison.
Nidorina
  • #030
  • The female Nidoran's evolved form. It is gentle and caring to its young.
Nidoqueen
  • #031
  • Its body is covered with needle-like scales. It never shows signs of shrinking from any attack.
Nidoran♂
  • #032
  • Stiffens its ears to sense danger. The larger its horns, the more powerful its secreted venom.
Nidorino
  • #033
  • An aggressive Pokémon that is quick to attack. Its horn contains venom.
Nidoking
  • #034
  • Uses its thick tail to constrict and break the bones of its prey.
Clefairy
  • #035
  • Its magical and cute appeal has many admirers. It is rare and found only in certain areas.
Clefable
  • #036
  • A timid fairy Pokémon that is rarely seen. It will run and hide the moment it senses people.
Vulpix
  • #037
  • At the time of birth, it has just one tail. The tail splits from its tip as it grows older.
Ninetales
  • #038
  • Very smart and very vengeful. Grabbing one of its many tails could result in a 1,000-year curse.
Jigglypuff
  • #039
  • When it wavers its big, round eyes, it begins singing a lullaby that makes everyone drowsy.
Wigglytuff
  • #040
  • Its body is very elastic. By inhaling deeply, it can continue to inflate itself without limit.
Zubat
  • #041
  • Forms colonies in perpetually dark places. Uses ultrasonic waves to identify and approach targets.
Golbat
  • #042
  • Once it strikes, it will not stop draining energy from the victim even if it gets too heavy to fly.
Oddish
  • #043
  • During the day, it stays in the cold underground to avoid the sun. It grows by bathing in moonlight.
Gloom
  • #044
  • The fluid that oozes from its mouth isn't drool. It is a nectar that is used to attract prey.
Vileplume
  • #045
  • Its petals are the largest in the world. As it walks, it scatters extremely allergenic pollen.
Paras
  • #046
  • Burrows to suck tree roots. The mushrooms on its back grow by drawing nutrients from the bug host.
Parasect
  • #047
  • A host-parasite pair in which the parasite mushroom has taken over the host bug. Prefers damp places.
Venonat
  • #048
  • Lives in the shadows of tall trees where it eats insects. It is attracted by light at night.
Venomoth
  • #049
  • The dust-like scales covering its wings are color-coded to indicate the kinds of poison it has.
Diglett
  • #050
  • A Pokémon that lives underground; it burrows to avoid sunlight.
Dugtrio
  • #051
  • A team of Diglett triplets. It triggers huge earthquakes by burrowing 60 miles underground.
